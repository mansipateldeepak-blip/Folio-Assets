Executive Snapshot

TL;DR
The Organization
The Ecosystem We Operate In

Infosys WingSpan - a White-label Digital Platform for Learning and Campus Ecosystems

Infosys developed WingSpan, a digital platform designed to power learning, operations, and engagement ecosystems for organizations and educational institutions. The platform was built as a white-label product — it could be adapted and branded for different universities or enterprises while sharing the same core technology infrastructure.

While the platform was internally used within Infosys under the name Lex, the broader WingSpan ecosystem was designed to support external institutions as well. To validate and expand the product's capabilities, Infosys partnered with universities to implement modules addressing campus operations and student services.

The First Implementation Client

One of the first universities to adopt the system was a large multi-campus institution with thousands of students distributed across multiple cities in India. The institution actively promotes sports, fitness, and wellness activities as part of student life. Each campus includes facilities such as gyms, yoga programs, sports activities, recreational clubs, and wellness centers. However, these services were still being managed through manual processes and fragmented coordination — making it an ideal environment to pilot and refine the platform.

The Project
System and Product Overview

A Digital Platform for Managing Campus Wellness and Recreational Activities

The product was a capacity-aware booking platform that allowed students to reserve access to campus facilities and activities such as gym sessions, yoga classes, and other wellness or recreational programs.

The system needed to support two fundamentally different booking models:

Facility Slot Booking

Continuous time slots across the day — e.g. gym access. Students book a time window, capacity is monitored in real time.

Scheduled Class Booking

Fixed sessions occurring at specific times and days — e.g. yoga or training sessions. Structured enrollment with defined capacity per session.

The platform included student portals (desktop web, responsive mobile, mobile app) and admin dashboards for managing schedules, capacities, and operational visibility. These booking models were designed to be scalable and reusable across other campus services — tennis courts, badminton courts, volleyball, chess, recreational workshops, and mental health consultations.

The Challenge
Where the System Was Breaking Down

When Student Motivation Meets Operational Chaos

Universities actively promoted sports, fitness, and wellness programs, but operational limitations prevented students from accessing them effectively. At peak hours, gym facilities faced long queues and overcrowding, while during other hours resources remained underutilized. Students often had no visibility into slot availability, leading to frustration and inefficient facility usage.

From the administrative side, managing schedules and capacities was largely manual, making it difficult to balance demand, ensure fairness, and monitor facility utilization.

The Core Problem
The issue wasn't simply booking slots. It was balancing demand and capacity across time.
Overcrowded peak hours — long queues, no advance booking
Underutilized off-peak capacity — same facilities sat nearly empty
No visibility into availability — students had no way to plan ahead

The university needed a system that could distribute demand more intelligently while keeping access fair for thousands of students.

The Solution
The Strategic Intervention

Designing a Capacity-Aware Booking System for Campus Activities

The product streamlined how campus wellness and recreational facilities were accessed and managed. Together, these systems ensured fair access for students while giving administrators better control over facility operations.

For Students

  • Smart slot booking for high-demand facilities like gyms
  • Structured class booking for scheduled activities such as yoga or training sessions
  • Real-time visibility of slot availability and capacity
  • A simple and consistent booking experience across devices

For Administrators

  • Admin dashboards to configure schedules, classes, and facility capacities
  • Operational visibility into slot utilization and peak demand
  • Tools to manage bookings, cancellations, and attendance
  • A scalable structure to support multiple campus services

Reflection
Where AI Could Have Accelerated This

This project predates widespread AI tooling (early 2023). Looking back, there are clear places where AI could have meaningfully reduced the manual effort required at this scale.

Where AI Could Have Helped
  • Sprint planning and team coordination at this scale required significant manual effort. AI could streamline task structuring, work distribution, and progress tracking across a multi-designer team
  • During ideation, exploring booking models, edge cases, and alternative flows required multiple rounds of manual iteration. AI could act as a rapid exploration partner, helping expand and validate directions faster
  • Early concept visualization was time-intensive, relying on sketches and gradual refinement. AI-assisted prototyping could accelerate this by quickly translating ideas into tangible interfaces
  • Documentation and iteration tracking across an 11-month build required continuous effort. AI could simplify this by structuring updates and maintaining design continuity
