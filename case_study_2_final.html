Chapter 0

Executive Snapshot

TL;DR
The Company
The Ecosystem We Operate In

Global Industrial Equipment Manufacturer Powering Warehouse Operations

A global manufacturer of industrial vehicles used in large warehouse and logistics operations. Its equipment powers distribution centers where fleet uptime, safety compliance, and operational visibility directly impact operational throughput.

To support modern warehouse environments, the company operates an enterprise telematics ecosystem that streams operational data from connected industrial vehicles deployed across customer sites. This digital infrastructure allows supervisors to monitor fleet activity, manage inspection workflows, and maintain operational control across large warehouse fleets.

The Project
System and Product Overview

Enterprise Telematics Platform for Fleet Operations

The organization operates an enterprise telematics platform used to monitor and manage fleets of connected industrial vehicles deployed across warehouse sites worldwide. The system aggregates operational data from 70,000+ connected vehicles, providing real-time visibility into vehicle usage, operational status, and safety processes.

Within this platform, supervisors rely on a high-frequency operational interface to monitor fleet activity and manage configuration workflows that support daily warehouse operations.

The Challenge
Where the System Was Breaking Down

High-frequency workflows had become slow and frustrating

Warehouse supervisors interacted with the system dozens of times each day to monitor fleet activity and manage operational processes. Over time, two critical workflows had become increasingly inefficient.

Vehicle Monitoring Page

The most frequently used interface displayed 45-50 configurable columns despite supervisors relying on only 7-8 key fields. Global search queried the entire vehicle dataset, creating severe performance bottlenecks.

  • 30-40 second load times on the vehicle monitoring page
  • 5-6 clicks required per checklist question

Checklist Creation Workflow

Creating inspection checklists required repeated popup interactions, turning a routine task into a time-consuming process.

  • ~100 clicks needed for a standard 20-question checklist
  • User progress lost if the workflow was interrupted

Early workflow mapping was done manually, while AI later assisted to structure and refine these flows more efficiently.

The Solution
The Strategic Intervention

Redesigning High-Frequency Operational Workflows for Usability and Performance

This project focused on redesigning two of the most frequently used workflows at the center of daily fleet operations. AI supported the process as a thinking partner - helping explore edge cases, interaction patterns, and validate solution directions.

For Vehicle Monitoring

The interface was restructured to prioritize the most relevant operational data, allowing supervisors to quickly scan fleet information without loading unnecessary columns.

Key Product Interventions
  • Single unified search bar replacing multiple search paths
  • Default view with the 7-8 most relevant columns
  • Custom search and saved search views
  • Search history for repeated operational queries
  • Table Settings to control visible columns
  • Grouped column categories (RTLS, operational signals, etc.)

For Checklist Creation

The workflow was redesigned into a single-page configuration experience, eliminating the need for repetitive popup interactions.

Key Product Interventions
  • Single-page checklist grid with inline editing
  • Save as Draft to prevent workflow interruption
  • Copy checklist to secondary language

The redesign focused on clarity, speed of interaction, and reduced cognitive load in an environment where these actions occur repeatedly throughout the day. AI was used selectively for UX copy, naming, and refining interaction details, helping reduce manual effort and improve clarity.

The Impact
What Changed - Measurably

The redesign transformed both workflows into significantly faster and simpler operational tools. Vehicle monitoring became faster and more focused by loading only relevant data fields, while checklist configuration moved from a repetitive interaction pattern to a streamlined editing experience.

Outcome

Supervisors gained faster access to fleet status and operational data, allowing them to monitor vehicles and identify issues more efficiently.

The platform shifted from a dense operational interface to a more streamlined workflow experience designed for high-frequency use.
